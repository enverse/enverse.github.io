Where do online GHG come from ?

It is becoming commong knowledge that our digital world is not dematerialised after all. 4% of worldwide GHG emissions come from the production and usage of our electronic gadgets. Let's have a look at where they come from.

If the internet was a country, it would be the 7th largest polluter in the world. It is predicted to double by 2025, and take up a staggering 14% of our total emissisions by 2040.

This is becoming more and more common knowledge, thanks ironically to Mr Musk, but also to valiant efforts to sensibilize the population to these issues (we strongly recommend the Fresque du Num√©rique workshop). But do you know where these emissions come from exactly ?

What can we do then ? According to all this data, we built three pillars when choosing a tech stack at Enverse Labs.

  • We believe in building energy efficient apps. Forget that bloated new js framework. We love tools that allow us to only send JS when we need (hello Astro.build and Next.JS pre-rendering)

  • We also have a responsibility to build retro-compatible applications, in order to allow people to keep using their devices for as long as possible ! Now that IE is dead, that are no more excuses üòõ

  • Build only what is nessecary ! A true agile MVP-first approach ensures that you only put out products that work, and avoid sending bloated software full of features no one will use !
