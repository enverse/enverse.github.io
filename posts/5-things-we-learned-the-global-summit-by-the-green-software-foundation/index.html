5 Things we Learned the Global Summit by the Green Software Foundation

At Enverse, we strive to be active members of the global green software movement. We know that strength is in numbers and we were excited to see what big players such as Microsoft and Accenture had to say about these issues that passionate us.

On june 15th 2022 we attended the Green Software Foundation's Global Summit. It was hosted virtually by the steering committee of the GSF.

We talked about green software, climate change, the current heatwaves in the world, and the impact that we as consumers of digital services have on our planet.

We summed up 5 key takeaways here that particularly struck us:

  1. Amount of work going into hardware

  2. Accessiblity has a role in eco-design

While a lot of the information presented was familiar to us, we ended up learning quite a lot. For example, did you know that over 50 different metals go into producing a laptop ? Or that 76% of the CO2 emissions of a Dell 7300 laptop come from the manufacturing process alone, and a staggering 47% for the printed wiring board ! That is even when we include energy usage over its lifetime.

Taking a holistic view, we (as developers) are still responsible for 30% of emissions.

Although it can be as little as 8% if we are in a country with very low carbon intensity power supplies (such as France or Canada):

So we should focus our efforts in making software that works well on older devices. Resist the temptation of offering features that only work on newer phones (hint: you probably don't need an NFC tag reading feature ;))

Low-tech solutions can also provide great ways to deliver value without encouraging your users to get a new phone.

An interesting takeaway was also that green software will be more accessible. This is an incredible selling point for software managers, as better accessibility means more people using your apps. Who wouldn't want that ?
